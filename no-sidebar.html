Bobby Flay

The life and lies of Food Network's most esteemed, ginger Iron-Chef.

Robert William Flay was born into a family of New York leprechauns on December 10, 1964. From the very beginning, little Bobby's parents just knew that he was a "special" one. Here are a few words from Bobby's great aunt Yaminetta Flay in an exclusive interview.
REPORTER: "So ma'am, we heard from a viable source that you were very close with your grand nephew during his earlier years. Has Robert always expressed interest in the culinary arts?"
YAMINETTA FLAY: "Oh you bet your alfafa buddy! I always said that my little Bobbo was born clutching spatulers in both hands, and egg beaters in both feet. He always did have freaky toes, but I suppose that's from my side of the family. (TEEHEE)"
REPORTER: "...Um. I'm really not sure what to say."
YAMINETTA FLAY: "Yup yup yup. Bobby's good eats always left me speechless too."
REPORTER: "Ah yes. Have you inspired, or passed down any family recipes to Mr. Flay?"
YAMINETTA FLAY: "I thought you'd never ask! In Bobby's restaurant in Vegas, La Mesa, there's a menu item named after me: The Yammy Jammy Hammy Surprise Hash! It's made of pureed yams, lingonberry jam, cinnamon-crusted ham, and lots and lots of sEcrEtS."
REPORTER: "Oh. Oh my. Well, I guess that concludes our exclusive interview with Yaminetta Flay. Honestly, if it lasts any longer I might throw up."
